Screening for survival-related gene panels in the Classical subtype

Among the three subtypes, only the Classical subtype has a poor prognosis sample, so the subtype may be screened for a gene panel that may be related to survival.

In statistics and machine learning, the Lasso algorithm (least absolute shrinkage and selection operator) is a simultaneous regression method for feature selection and regularization (mathematics). , designed to enhance the predictive accuracy and interpretability of statistical models. Lasso is a linear regression method using L1 regularization. Using L1 positive will make the partially learned feature weights 0, thus achieving the purpose of thinning and selecting variables and constructing the best model.

LASSO regression is characterized by variable selection and regularization while fitting a generalized linear model. Therefore, whether the dependent/response varaible is continuous or binary or discrete, it can be modeled and predicted using LASSO regression. Variable filtering here means not putting all the variables into the model for fitting, but selectively putting the variables into the model to get better performance parameters.

Methods:

single-factor Cox regression is performed on all differentially expressed genes in the classical subtype.

For the single factor significant gene, Lasso algorithm is used to select the best gene model based on COX multi-factor regression model.

Risk Index Analysis of Survival related Gene Panel in Classical subtype

For the gene panel obtained above, we calculated the Risk Score for each sample based on the expression and multivariate regression coefficients.

The samples were divided into high-index group and low-index group according to the risk index. Kaplan-Meier survival analysis was performed to plot the survival curve and the ROC curve of one-year, three-year and five-year survival.。

Survival Analysis of Gene Panel combined with clinical informaiotn in classical subtype

By plotting survival and ROC curves, we found that the gene panel risk index correlates with the survival of patients with a Classical subtype.

We further combined the clinical information of the gene panel, and performed a Cox regression univariate analysis based on the gene panel risk index and the patient's age, tumor primary tumor, lymph node involvement and immune index. Significant factors were selected for Cox multivariate regression.
