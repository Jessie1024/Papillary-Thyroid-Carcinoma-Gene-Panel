Analysis of genes and drugs related to lymph node involvement in Classical subtype

The Connectivity Map (cmap) ( https://portals.broadinstitute.org/cmap/ ) is a database of biological applications related to small molecule drugs, gene expression and disease. In the field of drug development, the rapid use of gene expression profiling data compares the drugs that are highly associated with disease, summarizing the possible mechanism of action of drug molecules.

The database is the difference in gene expression after treatment of human cells with small molecule drugs, and establishes the association between genes, diseases and drugs. There are currently 1,309 drug molecules accumulated, with a total of more than 7,000 gene expression profiles. Each drug molecule is treated in different cell lines at different concentrations and different treatment durations. The gene expression profile data is divided into a forward-regulated gene group and a negative-regulated gene group for analysis, and the degree of similarity of the gene map is mainly calculated, and finally the score is given. The closer the score is to 1, the positively related to the drug molecules of the two, called positive or induce connection; conversely, the drug molecule that is similar to the gene map of the negatively regulated gene group, the score will be negative, called negative or Reverse connection.

We used cmap to analyze highly associated drugs for genes involved in lymph node involvement in the Classical subtype..

Methods:

In the Classical subtype, based on the up-and-down expression of the lymph node involvement-related gene panel, cmap was used to analyze the gene expression pattern and small molecule drug correlation to find drugs that may be involved in lymph node involvement.

Results:

Aiming at the genes associated with regional lymph node involvement in the subclass of thyroid papillary metastasis, 19 significant related drug small molecules were found in 1309 drugs, of which the top ten were diethylstilbestrol, AH-6809, amantadine, ticarcillin, nifenazone, felbinac, imatinib. , CP-690334-01, anisomycin, finasteride.
